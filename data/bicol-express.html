Bicol Express

Ingredients

  • 500g pork belly (cut into thin strips or cubes)
  • 2 cups coconut milk (first extract or kakang gata, for a creamier texture)
  • 1 cup coconut cream (optional, for a thicker sauce)
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • 1 medium onion (chopped)
  • 4 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 2-3 tablespoons shrimp paste (bagoong alamang, adjust to taste)/li>
  • 10-12 pieces green finger chilies (siling haba, sliced diagonally)
  • 4-5 pieces red chilies (siling labuyo, chopped or crushed for added heat, optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the pork: In a large pan or wok, heat the oil over medium heat. Add the pork and cook until it turns light brown. Remove excess fat if necessary.
  2. Sauté aromatics: Push the pork to one side of the pan, then add onions and garlic. Sauté until fragrant and softened.
  3. Add shrimp paste: Stir in the shrimp paste and cook for about 2 minutes, ensuring the flavors meld with the pork.
  4. Pour in coconut milk: Add the coconut milk and bring it to a simmer. Lower the heat and let it cook for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the pork is tender and the sauce thickens.
  5. Add chilies: Add the green chilies and red chilies. Continue to cook until the sauce reduces further, about 10-15 minutes.
  6. Add coconut cream (optional): If you want a thicker, richer sauce, pour in the coconut cream and cook for another 5 minutes.
  7. Season and serve: Taste the dish and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper if necessary. Serve hot with steamed rice.
