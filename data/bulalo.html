Bulalo

Ingredients

  • 1 kg beef shank (with bone marrow)
  • 1 onion (quartered)
  • 1 corn on the cob (cut into 2-inch pieces)
  • 1 bunch pechay (bok choy)
  • 1 medium onion (chopped)
  • 2 potatoes (peeled and quartered)
  • 2-3 saba bananas (plantains, optional)
  • Water (enough to cover the meat)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fish sauce (optional)

Instructions

  1. Boil the beef shank: Place the beef shank in a large pot filled with water. Bring it to a boil and then reduce the heat to a simmer. Cook for 1.5 to 2 hours until the meat is tender, skimming off any impurities that rise to the surface to keep the broth clear and clean.
  2. Add aromatics and corn: Once the beef is tender, add the onion and corn to the pot. Continue to simmer for an additional 20-30 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the broth to become rich and savory.
  3. Incorporate the potatoes: Add the potatoes to the pot and cook until they are soft and easily pierced with a fork. This will add heartiness to the dish and balance the flavors.
  4. Season the broth: Taste the broth and season it with salt, pepper, and fish sauce if desired. This step enhances the overall flavor profile of the soup, making it more savory and satisfying.
  5. Add greens and bananas: Stir in the pechay (bok choy) and bananas, allowing them to simmer for just a few minutes. The greens will wilt slightly, and the bananas will soften, adding a unique sweetness to the dish.
  6. Serve and enjoy: Serve the hot soup with a side of calamansi and extra fish sauce, allowing everyone to customize their bowl for a burst of fresh flavor.
