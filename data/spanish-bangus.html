Spanish Bangus

Ingredients

  • 2 pieces ampalaya (bitter melon), thinly sliced (with seeds removed)
  • 200g shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1/4 cup water
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the ampalaya: Thinly slice the ampalaya (bitter melon) and soak the slices in a bowl of water with a little salt for 10-15 minutes. This process helps to reduce its bitterness. After soaking, drain and rinse the ampalaya under cold water to remove excess salt.
  2. Sauté the aromatics: In a pan, heat oil over medium heat. Add minced garlic, chopped onion, and diced tomatoes, sautéing until softened and fragrant. This will create a flavorful base for the dish.
  3. Cook the shrimp: Add the shrimp to the pan and cook until they turn pink and opaque, ensuring they are cooked through and tender.
  4. Add the ampalaya: Stir in the prepared ampalaya and stir-fry for about 3-4 minutes. Then, add a splash of water and allow it to simmer for another 5 minutes, or until the ampalaya is tender but still retains some crunch.
  5. Add the eggs: Pour in the beaten eggs, gently stirring the mixture until the eggs set and are fully cooked. This adds richness and texture to the dish.
  6. Season and serve: Taste the dish and season with salt and pepper according to your preference. Serve hot alongside rice for a delicious and nutritious meal.
