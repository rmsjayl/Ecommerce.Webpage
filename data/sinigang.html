Sinigang

(4.5/5 based on 200 reviews)

Ingredients

  • 500g pork belly or ribs (cut into chunks)
  • 1 packet sinigang mix (or fresh tamarind)
  • 1 onion (quartered)
  • 2 tomatoes (quartered)
  • 1 radish (sliced)
  • 1 bunch kangkong (water spinach)
  • 1 eggplant (sliced)
  • 2 green chilies
  • 2-3 cups water
  • Salt or fish sauce to taste

Instructions

  1. Boil the pork: In a large pot, combine pork with chopped onions and tomatoes. Add enough water to cover the ingredients and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat and simmer until the pork is tender, which usually takes about 1-1.5 hours, allowing the flavors to develop.
  2. Add the souring agent: Once the pork is tender, add the sinigang mix or fresh tamarind to the pot. Stir well and let it simmer, allowing the sour flavors to infuse into the broth.
  3. Incorporate the vegetables: Add the radish, eggplant, and chilies to the pot. Cook until the vegetables are soft, ensuring they absorb the delicious flavors of the broth.
  4. Add the greens: Gently stir in the kangkong (water spinach) and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, just until wilted and tender.
  5. Season to taste: Taste the soup and season with fish sauce according to your preference, enhancing the overall flavor profile of the dish.
  6. Serve and enjoy: Serve the sinigang hot, allowing the comforting and tangy broth to warm you up. It pairs perfectly with steamed rice for a complete meal.
